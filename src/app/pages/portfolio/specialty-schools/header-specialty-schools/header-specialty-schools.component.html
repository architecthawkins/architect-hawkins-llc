Specialty Schools
While I find that every project is unique – there are those projects that require a greater sensitivity and awareness in design, whether it is learning sign language to communicate, or kneeling down to see the world from a child’s eyes.


Phoenix Day School for the Deaf       Phoenix, AZ
51,286 SF
This two story facility services the deaf culture - spaces include administrative offices, the media center, and assembly space for the campus, and the middle school and high school classrooms for deaf students.

Laveen Elementary School
Laveen Elementary School District # 59       Laveen, AZ
96,613 SF
Children were envisioned as butterflies that bud and then fly away, this was the design concept on the west wall of the media center, in colorful small glass gems, that fluttered along a wall and disappear from sight. This concept of the selective use of color was used throughout the interior of the campus design. The exterior is contextual to the surrounding and blends into the mountain range.
Campus plan consisting of 4 buildings – Buildings A, B, C, D
Building A: Administrative Spaces & Media Center & Food Service
Building B: Pre-K & Kindergarten Classrooms
Building C: Gymnasium / Assembly Space for Campus
Building D: 2 story classroom wing for Grades 1 thru 8th
